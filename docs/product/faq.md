## Getting started

### How do I sign up?

Create an account from the **Sign up** page using your email and a password, or continue with Google. Once you're signed in, head to **Convert** to upload your first audio file.

### Can I use Google to sign in?

Yes. Both **Sign in with Google** and email/password are supported on the login and signup pages.

### I forgot my password — how do I reset it?

Go to the login page and choose **Forgot password**. We'll email you a time-limited link to set a new password.

## Audio uploads

### What audio formats are supported?

WhatSaid accepts `.m4a`, `.mp3`, and `.wav` files. `.m4a` (the default for Apple Voice Memos) is fully supported.

### What's the maximum file size and length?

Up to **100 MB** per file and up to **60 minutes** of audio per upload.

## Languages

### Which languages can WhatSaid transcribe?

WhatSaid auto-detects the spoken language during transcription. If detection picks the wrong language, you can override it manually before submitting the file.

### Can I force a specific language?

Yes. On the **Convert** page, set the language manually in the language selector before uploading. The override applies to that single job.

### Which interface languages are supported?

The app interface is available in **English, Italian, and French**. Switch from the language switcher in the navbar or from your account settings.

### Can I get the summary in a different language?

Yes. From the job page, you can generate and cache a translated version of an output (such as the summary) in another language.

## Transcripts and speakers

### Do transcripts include timestamps?

Yes. Every transcript is segmented with timestamps so you can navigate to any part of the recording.

### How accurate are transcripts?

Accuracy depends on audio quality and the language spoken. Clearly recorded speech in a supported language produces the best results. You can always edit the transcript inline to fix any mistakes.

### Can I correct mistakes in the transcript?

Yes. Open any transcript and edit it directly — your changes are saved to the job.

### Does WhatSaid identify different speakers?

Yes. When the recording has more than one voice, WhatSaid splits the transcript by speaker and assigns labels.

### Can I rename speakers?

Yes. Click any speaker label to rename it. WhatSaid will also suggest names automatically when the transcript provides enough context.

### How does WhatSaid guess who's speaking?

It uses the transcript itself — for example, when participants introduce themselves or address each other by name — to suggest speaker names you can accept or override.

## Summaries

### What does the summary include?

A structured summary with **key points** and **key actions** extracted from the transcript.

### How is the summary generated?

It's produced automatically from your transcript after transcription completes. You can read it on the **Summary** tab of any completed job.

### Does the summary update when I edit the transcript?

Yes. After editing the transcript, you can regenerate the summary so it reflects your changes (subject to a per-job regeneration limit).

## Questions about your transcripts

### What kind of questions can I ask?

Anything answerable from the transcript — for example, "What were the action items?", "What did the client object to?", or "Summarise the second half." Open the **Questions** tab on any completed job to ask.

### Are answers saved?

Yes. Every question and answer is saved with the job, so you can revisit them later.

### Can I edit or delete a saved question?

Yes. From the saved answer, edit the prompt and re-run it, or delete the entry entirely.

### Can I ask questions across several transcripts?

Yes. In the **Questions** tab, enable **Additional transcripts** and pick up to **5 other transcripts** you own. Your current transcript stays the primary source; the extras provide supporting context.

### Whose transcripts can I include as extras?

Only your own completed transcripts. Shared or other users' transcripts cannot be added as extra sources.

## Tags and organisation

### How are tags generated?

WhatSaid generates topical tags automatically from the transcript so you can group related jobs in your history.

### Can I add my own tags?

Yes. You can add or remove tags manually from the job page; your tags appear alongside the auto-generated ones.

---

## Titles and metadata

### Where do titles come from?

WhatSaid generates a short, descriptive title from the transcript automatically.

### Can I rename a job?

Yes. Click the title on the job page to rename it.

### Where does the recording date come from?

When the audio file carries embedded metadata (for example, recordings from iOS Voice Memos), WhatSaid extracts the original recording date and surfaces it on the job page.

### Why is no recording date shown?

Many files don't carry that metadata. When it's missing, WhatSaid falls back to the file's last-modified date or omits the field.

## Exports

### Which formats can I download?

You can export each transcript as **TXT**, **JSON**, **DOC**, or **PDF** from the **Export** menu on the job page.

### What's in the JSON export?

A structured representation of the job — transcript with timestamps and speakers, summary, and saved questions — suitable for processing in other tools.

### How long does the PDF take?

PDFs are generated in the background. You'll see progress in the notification bell at the top of the app.

### Where do I find the PDF once it's ready?

Open the notification bell — your PDF appears as a notification with a download link as soon as it's ready.

## Sharing

### How long does a share link last?

Share links expire after **2 days** and can be claimed once.

### Does the recipient need an account?

To **claim a copy** of the transcript, yes — they'll be prompted to sign in or sign up. To **download a one-off PDF** of a shared transcript, no account is required.

### Can someone download a PDF of a transcript I share?

Yes. The recipient gets a download link for a PDF of the shared transcript without needing to create an account.

## History

### Where do I find my past transcripts?

Open **History** from the navbar. You can search and filter by tag to find any past job.

### How long is history kept?

Your transcripts and outputs stay in your account indefinitely — only the original audio file is deleted after processing.

## Account

### How do I change my email?

Go to **Settings**, update the contact email, and confirm the change from the verification email we send.

### How do I change the app language?

From **Settings** (or the navbar language switcher), pick **English**, **Italian**, or **French**. Your choice is saved to your profile.

### How do I change my avatar?

Open **Profile** and upload a new image from the avatar editor.

### How do I delete my account?

Go to **Settings** and use the account deletion option in the danger zone. This permanently removes your account and associated data — it cannot be undone.

## Pricing and credits

### How many credits does a transcription cost?

Credits are charged in **15-minute brackets** based on the duration of the upload:

| Duration | Credits |
|---|---|
| Up to 15 min | 1 |
| Up to 30 min | 2 |
| Up to 45 min | 3 |
| Up to 60 min | 4 |

### How is duration measured?

By the actual length of your audio file — the bracket is determined when you upload.

### Which currencies are supported?

You can pay in **GBP**, **USD**, or **EUR**.

### How do I top up?

Buy credits in packs of **1, 5, or 20** from the **Pricing** page or the in-app top-up. Purchases are one-time — there are no subscriptions.

### Do credits expire?

Credits stay on your account; we don't auto-expire them. (For specific terms, see your purchase confirmation.)

---

## Privacy

### Do you store my audio?

No. Your audio is **deleted after processing**. Only the generated text — transcript, summary, questions, tags, and metadata — is kept in your account.

### How long is the audio kept?

Only as long as it takes to process the file. As soon as the job completes, the audio is deleted.
