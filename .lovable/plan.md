

# WhatSaid — Compliance, Privacy, and Legal Review

## A. Compliance Gaps and Risks (Current State)

| Gap | Severity | Current state |
|-----|----------|---------------|
| No recording-consent confirmation before upload | **High** | AudioUploader accepts files with zero consent acknowledgement |
| No Terms of Service or Privacy Policy | **Critical** | Footer has no links to legal pages; no ToS acceptance at signup |
| No AI-output accuracy disclaimer | **High** | Results shown without any caveat about AI limitations |
| No subprocessor/third-party disclosure | **High** | Audio is sent to AssemblyAI; users are not told |
| No guest data-access expiry or lifecycle | **Medium** | Guest job results stored indefinitely with no documented retention |
| No ToS checkbox on signup | **High** | Signup form collects email/password/name only |
| No cookie/analytics consent banner | **Medium** | None present (acceptable if no tracking, but should be explicit) |
| No DPIA or risk register | **Medium** | Audio + AI processing of speech = elevated risk under GDPR |
| Homepage privacy claim is absolute | **Medium** | "No storage. No retention." — transcripts ARE retained; this is misleading |
| No rate limiting on upload or edge functions | **Medium** | Abuse vector for cost and API exhaustion |
| No refund/failed-job policy | **Medium** | Credits are charged; no stated remedy for failures |
| No credit expiry policy | **Low** | Unstated whether credits expire |

## B. What Must Be Implemented in Product UX

### B1. Recording-consent confirmation (Convert page)
- **Mandatory checkbox** before the "Convert now" button: user must confirm they have the right to upload and process the recording.
- Shown to both guests and logged-in users, every time.
- Link to a "Recording guidelines" help section.

### B2. AI accuracy disclaimer (JobResults, JobDetail)
- Persistent small notice below every transcript/summary/custom output:
  *"This output was generated by AI and may contain errors. It should not be used as a verbatim legal or medical record."*

### B3. ToS + Privacy Policy acceptance (Signup page)
- Checkbox: "I agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy" with links.
- Block signup until checked.

### B4. Guest checkout consent (Convert page, guest flow)
- Before payment: "By proceeding, you agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy."

### B5. Footer legal links (all pages)
- Add: Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, (optionally) Acceptable Use Policy.

### B6. Correct homepage privacy messaging
- Change "No storage. No retention." to something accurate:
  *"Audio files are deleted immediately after processing. Transcripts and outputs are retained in your account."*
- For guests: clarify retention period.

### B7. Subprocessor disclosure (Privacy Policy + in-product)
- At minimum in Privacy Policy. Optionally a brief note on the Convert page:
  *"Audio is processed using third-party AI transcription services."*

### B8. Data export (Settings page)
- "Download my data" button for GDPR/CCPA subject access requests. Can be deferred post-MVP but should be planned.

### B9. Account deletion (Settings page)
- Already has delete account UI — verify it cascades and deletes all jobs, outputs, and profile data.

## C. What Must Be Added to Legal Documents/Policies

### C1. Privacy Policy (new page: `/privacy`)
Must cover:
- What data is collected (email, name, audio files, transcripts, outputs, payment metadata)
- Lawful basis (consent for processing, contract for service delivery, legitimate interest for fraud prevention)
- Audio retention: deleted immediately after processing
- Transcript/output retention: retained until account deletion (or N days for guests)
- Subprocessors: AssemblyAI (speech-to-text), Stripe (payments), Supabase/cloud provider (hosting/database)
- International transfers: AssemblyAI servers (US), Supabase region, Stripe
- User rights: access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection
- Cookie policy (if any tracking is added)
- Contact details for data requests

### C2. Terms of Service (new page: `/terms`)
Must cover:
- Acceptable use (no illegal recordings, no prohibited content)
- User responsibility for recording consent
- No guarantee of transcript accuracy
- Credit/payment terms and non-refundability (or refund conditions)
- Credit expiry policy (recommend: no expiry, or 12-month minimum)
- Service limitations and disclaimers
- Limitation of liability
- Governing law and jurisdiction
- Account termination rights

### C3. Acceptable Use Policy (can be a section within ToS)
- No uploading recordings made without consent of recorded parties
- No processing of content that violates applicable law
- No use for surveillance or covert monitoring
- Reserve right to suspend accounts for violations

## D. What Likely Needs Lawyer Review

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| Terms of Service draft | Enforceability, liability caps, jurisdiction clauses |
| Privacy Policy draft | GDPR/CCPA/UK-GDPR compliance varies by jurisdiction |
| Recording consent disclaimer wording | Must disclaim liability without implying legal advice |
| International data transfer mechanisms | SCCs, adequacy decisions — jurisdiction-specific |
| DPIA requirement | Mandatory under GDPR Art. 35 if processing voice data at scale |
| Refund policy | Consumer protection laws differ (EU 14-day withdrawal, US state laws) |
| Credit expiry | Some jurisdictions treat prepaid credits as stored value with consumer protections |
| Guest data retention period | Must be defensible under data minimisation principles |

**Recommendation**: Draft all documents in-house, then have a privacy lawyer review before public launch. Do not claim GDPR compliance without jurisdiction-specific review.

## Jurisdiction-Sensitive Warnings

- **Recording consent laws vary dramatically**: one-party vs. all-party consent (US states differ; EU generally requires all-party). WhatSaid must NOT claim that a simple checkbox makes recordings legal. The product should disclaim: *"You are responsible for complying with recording laws in your jurisdiction."*
- **GDPR**: If serving EU users, voice data is personal data. Full GDPR compliance required including lawful basis, DPO consideration, DPIA, and data subject rights.
- **CCPA/CPRA**: If serving California residents, privacy notice must include specific disclosures.
- **Children**: If there's any possibility of minors' voices in recordings, additional protections may apply (COPPA, GDPR Art. 8). Recommend adding a ToS clause: service is for users 18+.
